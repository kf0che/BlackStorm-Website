# Alerting and Notifications

## Purpose

Alerts should help customers and support teams take useful action. They should not create panic, confusion, or unnecessary interruptions.

## Alert Levels

### Informational

Routine notices, completed checks, or low-priority changes.

### Needs Review

Items that may need attention but do not necessarily indicate an outage or emergency.

### Important

Issues that may affect service quality, device availability, or customer operations.

### Critical

Issues that need timely review and clear ownership.

## Notification Principles

- Use plain language.
- Include the affected service or device when available.
- Explain whether action is needed.
- Group repeated alerts where possible.
- Avoid implying guaranteed prevention or guaranteed uptime.
