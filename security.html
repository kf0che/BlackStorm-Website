Security & Compliance

Security-Focused.
Compliance-Aware.

BlackStorm operates with security as a foundational principle — not an afterthought. Our processes, systems, and services are designed with NIST Framework guidance and CISA best practices in mind.

Compliance Transparency Notice: BlackStorm, LLC is a compliance-aware technology services company. We do not claim regulatory certifications (such as SOC 2, HIPAA, or PCI-DSS) on behalf of clients and do not act as a compliance auditor. Our services are designed with industry best practices in mind to support clients in building a stronger security and operational posture.
Our Approach

Security Is Built In, Not Bolted On

Every BlackStorm service — from monitoring configuration to data handling to portal access controls — is designed with security considerations from the ground up. We don't add security features after the fact; we start every design decision by asking: how do we do this safely?

This matters because your infrastructure data is sensitive. Your client environments are sensitive. The visibility BlackStorm provides into your network must itself be protected, controlled, and auditable.

  • Access controls enforced at every portal layer
  • Data handled with privacy and confidentiality by default
  • Separation of client environments in multi-tenant contexts
  • Operational logging and audit trails for visibility
  • Encrypted connections for all data in transit
Security-First Design Philosophy

We evaluate security implications before finalizing any architectural or operational decision. If something can't be done securely, we don't ship it.

Least Privilege Access

Users and systems receive only the minimum permissions required to perform their function. No over-permissioning by default.

Framework Alignment

Designed with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework in Mind

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework is the industry-standard model for understanding, managing, and reducing cybersecurity risk. BlackStorm's service design maps to each of its five core functions.

Identify

We help clients understand what assets exist in their environment and which are critical — a foundational step for any security posture. Our monitoring onboarding includes environment mapping and device inventory.

Protect

Our services, portals, and infrastructure are designed with protective controls from day one — access management, data handling policies, and encryption are standard, not optional.

Detect

This is the core of what BlackStorm does. Continuous monitoring, anomaly alerting, and threshold-based notifications give clients the detection capability that makes response possible.

Respond

Alerts are designed to give responders the information they need — what failed, when, and for how long. Our incident history, recovery notifications, and escalation workflows support faster response.

Recover

Historical monitoring data provides a baseline for recovery efforts and post-incident analysis. Understanding what normal looks like makes restoring normal faster and more defensible.

CISA Guidance

Informed by Federal Cybersecurity Best Practices

CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency) is the US federal authority on cybersecurity guidance for critical infrastructure and commercial organizations. BlackStorm uses public CISA guidance as a reference point for security-aware operations.

When CISA publishes an advisory about a widespread threat or newly discovered vulnerability, that guidance can help inform monitoring conversations and infrastructure posture recommendations when relevant to a client environment.

  • Awareness of CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV)
  • CISA advisory awareness for relevant client conversations
  • Alignment with CISA's Secure by Design principles
  • Infrastructure practices consistent with CISA hardening guidance

CISA Secure by Design

Technology products should be secure out of the box. BlackStorm's portals and monitoring tools are built with this principle as a standard expectation, not a premium feature.

Known Exploited Vulnerabilities

CISA's KEV catalog helps inform conversations about actively exploited vulnerabilities that may be relevant to commonly monitored devices.

Zero Trust Awareness

Our access control design is informed by Zero Trust principles — verify identity, limit access, log everything, trust nothing implicitly.

Audit Readiness

Supporting Your Audit-Ready Direction

While BlackStorm is not a compliance auditor, our monitoring and visibility services create the documented operational record that compliance conversations require.

Performance History

Documented uptime, latency, and incident history for every monitored device — creating an operational record over time.

Incident Documentation

Every alert and recovery event is logged with timestamps, device identification, and duration — ready for review when needed.

Access Controls

Role-based access management with logged authentication — demonstrating controlled access to infrastructure data.

Uptime Reporting

Professional uptime attainment reports that document monitored availability over time — useful for client, leadership, and auditor review.
