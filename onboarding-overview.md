# Onboarding Overview

BlackStorm onboarding should help customers start using network monitoring without feeling overwhelmed.

The onboarding experience should feel calm, practical, and guided. Customers should know what information BlackStorm needs, why it matters, and what happens next.

## Onboarding Goals

The onboarding system should:

- Explain each setup step in plain language
- Collect only the information needed to begin monitoring
- Help customers identify important devices and sites
- Set expectations for alerts and reports
- Introduce support paths early
- Connect customers to learning resources at the right moment

## Intended Customer Psychology

Customers may arrive with uncertainty:

- "I do not know what devices matter."
- "I am not technical enough for this."
- "Will this be hard to set up?"
- "What happens if I make a mistake?"

The onboarding experience should answer those fears with structure, reassurance, and clear next steps.

## Accessibility Goals

Onboarding should support:

- Keyboard navigation
- Screen reader-friendly step labels
- Clear form labels
- Plain error messages
- Visible progress
- Save-and-resume workflows in the future
- Mobile-friendly layouts

## Progressive Disclosure Strategy

Show only what the customer needs at each step. Deeper technical explanations should be available through help text, tooltips, and contextual panels.

Do not present every possible configuration option at once.

## Future Scaling Goals

The onboarding foundation should support future:

- Customer training systems
- Certification or completion tracking systems
- Interactive troubleshooting
- Operational education modules
- Guided labs
- Localization
