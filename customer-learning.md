# Customer Learning

Customer learning is the practical education layer that helps users understand BlackStorm monitoring and portal workflows.

Learning content should be modular, searchable, and connected to the task the customer is trying to complete.

## Learning Content Types

### Educational Cards

Short cards that introduce a topic.

Examples:

- What is a monitored device?
- What does "Needs Review" mean?
- Who should receive alerts?
- How do recovery notifications work?

### Guided Learning Modules

Multi-step lessons for larger topics.

Examples:

- Getting started with monitoring
- Understanding your first alert
- Reviewing an uptime summary
- Creating a support ticket

### Knowledge Base Articles

Longer articles for searchable support content.

Examples:

- How alert notifications work
- How to read device status
- What BlackStorm can and cannot monitor
- How to update contact information

### Troubleshooting Walkthroughs

Future guided flows for common issues.

Examples:

- A device is offline
- Alerts are going to the wrong person
- A device recovered before I checked it
- I do not know my device IP address

## Customer Learning Goals

Customer learning should help users:

- Understand monitoring concepts
- Complete setup tasks
- Interpret alerts
- Use the portal confidently
- Ask better support questions
- Share clearer information with their team

## Mobile Usability

Learning content should be readable on a phone:

- Short headings
- Single-column cards
- No horizontal scrolling except data tables when necessary
- Captions for media
- Clear "continue" and "back" actions

## Future Interactive Labs

Interactive labs may eventually let customers practice:

- Reading an alert
- Identifying a device
- Choosing alert recipients
- Reviewing a ticket timeline
- Understanding a status summary
