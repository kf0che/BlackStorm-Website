# Support Workflows

BlackStorm support workflows should help customers ask for help without confusion.

The support experience should be calm, structured, and transparent. Customers should know what information to provide and what happens next.

## Support Workflow Goals

Support workflows should:

- Let customers create support tickets
- Show open and recent tickets
- Connect tickets to monitored devices or sites where relevant
- Show ticket status clearly
- Support customer replies and updates
- Make escalation paths clear without overpromising response guarantees

## Ticket States

Suggested ticket states:

- New
- Open
- Waiting on BlackStorm
- Waiting on customer
- Monitoring
- Resolved
- Closed

Status colors should be calm and paired with text labels.

## Ticket Panel Fields

Ticket panels may show:

- Ticket ID
- Subject
- Customer site
- Related device
- Status
- Last updated
- Assigned contact
- Next step

## Creating a Ticket

Customers should be asked for:

- Subject
- Description
- Site or location
- Related device, if relevant
- Urgency
- Preferred contact method
- Attachments, if supported

Customers should be reminded not to submit passwords or secrets.

## Customer Trust Considerations

Support copy should avoid sounding automated or dismissive.

Good support language:

- "We received your request."
- "We need one more detail from you."
- "This ticket is linked to an active device alert."
- "This appears recovered, but we are keeping the ticket open for review."

Avoid:

- Overly technical ticket states
- Panic-heavy labels
- Promised response times unless backed by a written agreement

## Accessibility Considerations

Support workflows should:

- Use accessible form labels
- Provide clear error messages
- Avoid placeholder-only form labels
- Support keyboard submission
- Confirm successful ticket creation
- Keep ticket status text visible
