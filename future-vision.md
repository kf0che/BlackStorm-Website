# BlackStorm Future Vision

BlackStorm, LLC is building from a focused starting point: practical network monitoring and infrastructure visibility.

The future vision is to improve the way small businesses, MSPs, and growing organizations understand their technology environments without turning the website into a list of unsupported promises.

## Current Foundation

The current foundation is the Network Monitoring Service.

This service helps customers:

- Track device availability
- Receive alerts
- Review outage and recovery history
- Understand basic uptime patterns
- Share clearer information with stakeholders

Future work should strengthen this foundation rather than distract from it.

## Guiding Principles for Future Services

### Build Before Announcing

BlackStorm should not market future services as available until they are built, tested, and ready.

### Label Maturity Clearly

Future initiatives should be marked as:

- Available
- In development
- Planned
- Researching
- Not currently available

### Keep the Customer in Control

Future tools should help customers understand their own environments and make better decisions.

### Avoid Unsupported Claims

Future content should not imply certifications, compliance levels, customer relationships, operating scale, or service maturity that does not exist.

### Better Reporting

Future reporting improvements may include:

- Monthly uptime summaries
- Incident timelines
- Device trend reports
- Plain-language executive summaries

Reports should remain operational summaries, not compliance certifications.

### Security-Aware Visibility

BlackStorm may explore ways to help customers better understand network exposure, device visibility, and operational risk.

This should remain carefully worded. Security-aware visibility is not the same as a certification, audit, penetration test, or legal compliance service.

## What Is Not Included in the Vision

BlackStorm should not include unrelated standalone projects in the website roadmap.

Future initiatives should stay connected to network monitoring, customer visibility, reporting, MSP workflows, support, and security-aware operational guidance.

Future initiatives should not overstate relationships, certifications, customer maturity, or technical capabilities.
